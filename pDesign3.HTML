GivingFoodies Mobile Wireframe

About

The app aims to bridge the gap between food surplus and food insecurity in Singapore by connecting dealers with those in need. Users can easily list the surplus food (e.g. from their household, restaurants, or supermarkets) while other users can browse and claim available items in real-time.

Goals

The product goal for this project is "To improve the accessibility of food/beverages and or leftover foods for those in need around the community". In addition, I wanted the app to be inclusive and cater to everyone in the community. To achieve this, I focused on creating a user-friendly interface that is intuitive and accessible to a wide range of users.

Process

To design my app, I began with UI research and competitor analysis to assess the strengths and weaknesses of existing solutions. In the first slideshow, I highlighted two popular apps used in Singapore for recycling food and identified areas for improvement. Next, I created User Personas to better understand the (type of) target audience. Lastly, using a sitemap, I was able to visualize the app’s layout and potential pages, which helped clarify the structure and user flow.

Challenges

One challenge I encountered was implementing interactions in the wireframe. It was my first time using Figma’s Open Overlay function to display pop-ups after successful actions. Initially, I duplicated pages to add pop-ups, but I later discovered that overlays were a more efficient method. This approach streamlined the wireframe and reduced repetition.

Another challenge was using variables and conditionals to enhance the app experience. It required careful planning to determine where and how to apply them effectively. I reference YouTube tutorials to better understand the conditional function, which ultimately allowed me to create a more dynamic and engaging user experience.

Results

In the end, I was able to deliver a user-friendly application for users in the community. This could potentilally reduce food wastage and enforces a sharing culture amongst individuals and businesses to contribute to a zero-waste future while helping to feed those vulnerable in the community.
